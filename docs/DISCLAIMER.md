# Disclaimer

## What TAI KIT is

TAI KIT is an open collection of 3D models depicting everyday Taiwanese-style
kitchen objects — bowls, cups, pots, utensils, and other generic household
kitchenware. The meshes are generated by AI image-to-3D reconstruction from
single-view photographs of generic utilitarian items.

## What TAI KIT is not

- **Not 3D scans** of physical objects. These are reconstructions generated
  by neural networks. They are visually plausible but geometrically
  approximate. Shape, proportion, and hidden (back-side) detail may differ
  from any real-world referent.
- **Not photogrammetry** in the classical multi-view sense.
- **Not affiliated** with any manufacturer, retailer, importer, designer,
  distributor, or catalog.
- **Not a representative sample** of any specific brand's product line.

## Naming and attribution

Every item in this release is given a **generic functional descriptor**
composed from detected attributes (category, material, color, style). Names
and metadata contain no brand, SKU, product code, designer attribution,
or source URL. Original filenames and any link to the underlying photography
are intentionally withheld.

If you recognize an object as resembling a specific commercial product, that
resemblance is coincidental to its role as an everyday utilitarian item.
Specific brand identities are not claimed, represented, or endorsed.

## On copyright of depicted objects

Most kitchen utensils are **functional items**. Under Taiwanese and most
international copyright frameworks, the inherent utility of a functional
object excludes it from copyright protection as an artistic work. Design
patent protection, where it exists at all, is limited in duration (15 years
in Taiwan) and applies only to registered ornamental features.

We release the 3D mesh geometry under the MIT License as our own AI-generated
expression. We make **no claim** over any underlying physical design, nor do
we warrant that downstream commercial use is free of third-party design
rights in every jurisdiction. Users are responsible for their own legal
diligence.
